The Wonder Bowl - Lesson Summary
7th Standard English (Second Language) - Prose Chapter 5
Karnataka State Board (KSEEB)

---

The old couple, Philemon and Baucis, lived in a hut on a mound. They worked hard on their farm and were only able to get as much food as they needed. Yet they were so kind that they would never let any stranger who came to their doors go without food. They welcomed every guest with joy and shared their own meal with him.

One evening, after finishing their supper, they sat at the door. Suddenly, they heard the barking of dogs and shouting of boys in the village. Philemon saw two strangers at whom the village boys hooted and the dogs barked. Philemon went down to meet the ragged newcomers. He drove back the dogs and said, "Welcome strangers." He took them into his poor cottage and made them sit at the table.

Baucis placed a small bowl of milk on the table and said, "This is all that I have for you, sirs. How I wish you had come a little earlier! We would have shared our supper with you."

Baucis then poured the milk from the bowl into two cups and placed them before the guests. They drank the milk and later one of them asked for some more. Baucis said, "There is hardly a drop left in this bowl!" "Why?" cried the guest taking the bowl, "There is milk in this bowl as much as we want." So saying, he filled not only his cup but also his friend's. The bowl kept refilling itself miraculously.

"They are not ordinary men," whispered Baucis to her husband. "Yes," he replied, "they must be angels from heaven, come to bless us!"

Philemon and Baucis could hardly sleep that night because of the wonder that the strangers had worked with the bowl. The guests arose early next morning and got ready to go. Philemon and Baucis walked out with them for a short distance.

"Good Philemon and Baucis," said one of the guests, "We are God's messengers. We are much pleased with your kindness. Ask for anything you like. You shall have it!"

After a moment Philemon said, "Good angels, we wish to die together." "Be it so," replied the angels. Old Philemon and Baucis were astonished to see their poor cottage turned into a grand mansion. When they turned round to thank the angels, they saw nobody there. They humbly prayed to God and lived in their mansion doing good to everyone.

---

Moral of the Story:
Kindness and hospitality are always rewarded. True generosity means sharing what little you have with others. Philemon and Baucis, despite being poor, showed great kindness to strangers, and they were blessed in return.
