* Teacher (ಗುರು) explains — experienced, clear, warm
* Student (ವಿದ್ಯಾರ್ಥಿ) asks questions, summarizes, reacts — curious, engaged

This is REVISION, not first-time teaching.

Do NOT pad or stretch. Duration depends on content density:

* Light concept (1 definition, 0-1 formulas, 1 example): 2–3 minutes
* Medium concept (2-3 definitions, 1-2 formulas, 2-3 examples): 4–6 minutes
* Heavy concept (3+ definitions, 2+ formulas, theorems, 3+ examples): 7–10 minutes

Approximate: 150 Kannada words = 1 minute of spoken audio.
Count your total script words and estimate duration. Be honest.

RULES FOR "dialogues" ARRAY:
- Each object is ONE person speaking ONE turn
- "index" starts at 1, increments by 1
- "speaker": "teacher" or "student"
- "section": one of "intro", "definition", "property", "formula", "theorem", "example", "exam_tips", "recap"
- "script": ONLY field. TTS-ready. ZERO math symbols. Pure spoken Kannada words.
- Each turn: 20–80 words. Not too short (choppy), not too long (monologue)
- Teacher can speak 2-3 turns in a row during complex explanations — NOT strict ping-pong

Teacher: Confirms, adds key detail
(Skip if no definitions in module JSON)

[PROPERTIES] — 2-4 dialogues
Teacher: Lists key properties with quick numerical examples
Student: Asks about edge case or summarizes
(Skip if no properties in module JSON)

[FORMULAS] — 3-6 dialogues per formula
Teacher: Names formula, reads in spoken words, explains each part
Student: Asks when to use, or about exception
Teacher: Answers with quick mental-math example using ACTUAL numbers
(Skip if no formulas in module JSON)

[THEOREMS] — 3-6 dialogues per theorem
Teacher: States in simple terms, explains what it MEANS practically
Student: Asks about common mistakes
Teacher: Warns about traps, mentions converse
(Skip if no theorems in module JSON)

[WORKED EXAMPLE] — 4-8 dialogues per example (pick best 1-2)
Teacher: States problem in words
Student: Identifies what is given and what to find
Teacher: Walks through steps with ACTUAL numbers spoken out
Student: Asks about a step or confirms understanding
Teacher: States answer
Student: Mentions trap if one exists
(Skip if no worked_examples in module JSON)

[EXAM TIPS] — 2-4 dialogues
Student: Asks how this appears in GPSTR exam
Teacher: Shares weightage, MCQ pattern, key trap
Student: Summarizes biggest thing to remember

[RECAP] — 2-3 dialogues
Teacher: Rapid 3-5 point summary
Student: Restates the ONE most important thing
Teacher: Encouraging sign-off
