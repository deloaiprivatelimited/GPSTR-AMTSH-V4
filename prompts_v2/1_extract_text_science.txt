FORMATTING RULES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. Preserve the original Kannada text as-is — do NOT translate or paraphrase
2. Mark headings clearly:
   # Chapter Title
   ## Section Heading
   ### Subsection Heading

3. Write all scientific formulas in LaTeX:
   Inline: $formula$
   Display: $$formula$$

4. Chemical equations in LaTeX:
   $$2H_2 + O_2 \rightarrow 2H_2O$$
   Use \rightarrow for yields, \rightleftharpoons for reversible reactions

5. Mark worked examples clearly:
   --- EXAMPLE START ---
   Source: Class {X}, Example {N} (page approximate)
   Problem: ...
   Solution:
   Step 1: ...
   Step 2: ...
   Answer: ...
   --- EXAMPLE END ---

6. Mark scientific laws/principles clearly:
   --- LAW ---
   Name: ...
   Statement: ...
   Formula: ... (if applicable)
   --- END LAW ---

7. Mark definitions:
   --- DEFINITION ---
   Term: ...
   Definition: ...
   --- END DEFINITION ---

8. Mark experimental activities:
   --- ACTIVITY ---
   Title: ...
   Materials: ...
   Procedure: ...
   Observation: ...
   Conclusion: ...
   --- END ACTIVITY ---

9. Tables as markdown:
   | Header 1 | Header 2 |
   |----------|----------|
   | data     | data     |

10. Figures — describe what you SEE:
    --- FIGURE ---
    Description: [describe the scientific diagram, apparatus, biological structure, circuit, etc.]
    Labels: [list ALL labeled parts, measurements, arrows, flow directions]
    Scientific significance: [what concept this figure illustrates]
    --- END FIGURE ---

11. Important boxes / remarks:
    --- NOTE ---
    (content)
    --- END NOTE ---
